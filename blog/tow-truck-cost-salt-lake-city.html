How Much Does a Tow Truck Cost in Salt Lake City? (2026 Pricing Guide)

Broken down in SLC and wondering what a tow will run you? Here's everything you need to know about towing costs in Salt Lake City — from local hops to long-distance hauls.

📅 Updated March 17, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ SLC Tow Trucks Editorial Team

⚡ Quick Answer

Most tows in Salt Lake City cost $75–$150 for the first 5 miles, plus $2–$5 per mile after that. Emergency and after-hours calls add $25–$75. Flatbed towing runs $100–$250. Long-distance towing (e.g., SLC to Provo or Ogden) typically costs $150–$350.

What Affects the Price of a Tow in SLC?

1. Distance

Distance is the biggest factor. Most companies charge a flat "hook-up fee" (usually $50–$100) plus a per-mile rate. A short tow from your apartment to the nearest shop might cost $75. A haul from downtown SLC to Park City (35+ miles) could run $175–$300.

2. Type of Tow Truck

There are three main types you'll encounter in SLC:

  • Wheel-lift: The cheapest option — works for most standard cars and trucks.
  • Flatbed: Safer for all-wheel drive, luxury, or damaged vehicles. Costs $30–$75 more.
  • Heavy-duty: For trucks, RVs, commercial vehicles. Premium rates apply.

Pro tip: If you drive an AWD vehicle (Subaru, Audi, Jeep), always request a flatbed. Wheel-lifting an AWD car can damage the drivetrain.

3. Time of Day

Calls between 10 PM and 6 AM typically incur an after-hours surcharge. Expect to pay $25–$75 more for a 2 AM breakdown. Some 24/7 companies in SLC have flat rates regardless of time — worth asking before they dispatch.

4. Location

If you break down in a difficult spot — on a canyon road, in a tight parking structure, off a highway embankment — expect a "special conditions" surcharge. Tows from Parleys Canyon or Big Cottonwood Canyon often cost more due to limited access and equipment needs.

5. Your Insurance or Roadside Plan

This is the biggest money-saver. Many drivers don't realize their insurance already covers towing:

  • AAA: Covers tows up to 5 miles (Basic), 100 miles (Plus), or 200 miles (Premier)
  • Most major auto insurers (State Farm, GEICO, Progressive, USAA) offer roadside assistance add-ons for $5–$15/year that cover towing
  • Credit cards: Some Visa/MasterCard/Amex cards include roadside assistance

Always call your insurer or AAA first — a covered tow costs you nothing.

How to Avoid Getting Overcharged

Unfortunately, towing scams exist everywhere — including SLC. Here's how to protect yourself:

  1. Get a price quote before they hook up. Ask: "What's the total cost for [destination]?" Reputable companies give upfront quotes.
  2. Use a directory. Companies listed on verified directories like SLC Tow Trucks have agreed to transparent pricing and are easier to hold accountable.
  3. Ask about after-hours fees. Make sure they disclose any surcharges before dispatch.
  4. Check reviews. A company with 200+ reviews and 4.5+ stars is unlikely to scam you.
  5. Know Utah's towing laws. Utah has towing rate regulations. If you're towed from private property without consent, the towing company is required to accept your payment card and must provide an itemized bill.

What's Included in a Standard SLC Tow?

When you call a tow truck in Salt Lake City, a standard service typically includes:

  • Dispatching a truck to your location
  • Loading your vehicle safely
  • Transport to your specified destination (within agreed distance)
  • Basic communication about ETA

Things that may cost extra: waiting time (if you're not there when they arrive), special equipment, more than one destination, and storage fees if your vehicle sits at their lot overnight.

Cheap Towing Options in Salt Lake City

Need to save money? Here are the best options for budget towing in SLC:

  • Compare multiple companies — Use the SLC Tow Trucks directory to compare multiple providers quickly.
  • AAA membership — At $70–$130/year, AAA pays for itself the first time you need a tow.
  • Add roadside to your insurance — Usually under $15/year with major carriers.
  • Call during business hours — Avoid after-hours surcharges if the situation isn't urgent.
  • Short tow to a closer shop — Rather than towing 20 miles to your usual mechanic, tow 2 miles to the nearest shop and transfer later.

Bottom Line

A typical tow in Salt Lake City costs $75–$150 for local distances. Flatbed tows, canyon rescues, and after-hours calls push that higher. If you have insurance roadside coverage or AAA, use it — it's already paid for.
