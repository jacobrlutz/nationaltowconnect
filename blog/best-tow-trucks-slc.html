How to Choose a Tow Truck Company in SLC

Check for Licensing and Insurance

In Utah, tow truck operators must be licensed by the Utah Department of Transportation (UDOT). Always ask if the company is licensed. Our directory marks verified companies with a green ✓ Verified badge — we've confirmed their licensing status.

Get a Quote Before They Dispatch

Reputable towing services in Utah will give you a price estimate before the truck rolls. The typical cost for a standard tow in Salt Lake City ranges from $75–$125 for local tows and $3–$5 per mile for longer distances. Be wary of companies that refuse to quote until after the tow.

Confirm Response Time

Ask for an estimated arrival time when you call. If the company won't commit to a window, that's a yellow flag. Most reputable SLC towing companies can give a 15–45 minute estimate depending on your location and time of day.

24/7 vs. Limited Hours

Not every company operates around the clock. If you're calling after midnight or on a holiday, make sure you're calling a 24 hour towing Salt Lake City provider. Filter for 24/7 service in our listings directory.

Emergency Towing in SLC: What to Do

  1. Get to safety first. If you can, move your vehicle to the shoulder or a parking lot. Turn on hazard lights.
  2. Call for a tow — use our directory to find a 24/7 provider near you.
  3. Note your location — nearest cross street, mile marker on the highway, or drop a pin in your phone's maps app.
  4. Stay in your vehicle if on a highway, especially at night. Wait with doors locked until help arrives.
  5. Don't accept unsolicited help from drivers who "just happen to be passing." Stick with the company you called.

  • Local tow (under 5 miles): $75–$100
  • Mid-distance (5–20 miles): $100–$175
  • Long-distance (20+ miles): $3–$5/mile + hook fee
  • Lockout service: $50–$85

  • Flat tire change: $55–$80
  • Fuel delivery (1 gal): $50–$70 + fuel cost

Prices vary by company and may be higher late at night, on holidays, or for specialty vehicles. Always confirm pricing before work begins.
